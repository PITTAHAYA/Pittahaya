The land

Flavor begins much earlier.

“Quality begins with the people who understand the land.”

We work with growers who have spent three generations reading the climate, humidity, and exact point of fermentation. We do not buy cacao: we buy judgment. The rest—the roasting, conching, and tempering—can only preserve what they have already achieved.

Why origin changes flavor.

One country, four climates. Altitude, rainfall, and soil leave a signature you can recognize in the cup.

Manabí

ClimateDry, with an ocean breeze ProfileNut, caramel, firm body HarvestMarch–June

Sandy soil and lower rainfall force the tree to concentrate. The result is a firm-bodied cacao with toasted sweetness and low acidity.

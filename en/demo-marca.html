02 — The System

What holds the five worlds together.

None of those worlds started from scratch. They all emerge from the same decisions: a type scale, spacing rhythm, grid, and color rule. Here it is, live — and X-Ray lets you see it over every part of the page.

One axis: editorial serif for what moves, grotesque for what informs, and monospaced for what measures.

Color · foundations and accent

Black and ivory do 95% of the work. Fruit and lime appear only where attention belongs. An accent used too often stops being an accent.

Rhythm · 8 px base

Every spacing value is a multiple of eight. It sounds rigid; that is exactly what makes five different worlds feel made by the same hand.

--ritmo: 8px · 8 · 16 · 24 · 40 · 56 · 72 · 88 · 96

Grid · 12 columns

Twelve columns because they divide cleanly into 2, 3, 4, and 6. The margin breathes with the viewport; the gutter does not. Turn on X-Ray and you will see the grid fall across everything else.

--col: 12 · --gutter: clamp(12,1.6vw,24) · --margen: clamp(20,5vw,72) · --maxw: 1460

The grid is not portfolio decoration: it is why a client can request a new page two years later and have it fit without debate.

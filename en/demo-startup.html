The thesis

Earth has run out of
energy, water, and land.

A gigawatt data center consumes as much electricity as a midsize city and evaporates millions of liters of water each year for cooling. In low Earth orbit, all three problems disappear at once.

01 / Energy

Sun without night

In a Sun-synchronous orbit, the panels receive constant irradiance with no atmosphere to filter it. No day-night cycle, no clouds, no seasonality.

1,361W/m² · solar constant
02 / Cooling

The vacuum is the heat sink

Deep space is at 2.7 K. Deployable radiators emit waste heat directly as infrared radiation. No cooling towers, no chillers, not a single drop of water.

0liters of water per year
03 / Latency

Light travels faster in a vacuum

In silica fiber, light travels at 67% of c. In a vacuum, it travels at 100%. Across continental distances, intersatellite optical links beat terrestrial fiber.

+47% faster than fiber

The unit

One node. 1.2 petaflops.
Fourteen years without maintenance.

Each node is a closed system: compute, power, thermal control, and optical link in a single radiation-tolerant module. It launches folded, deploys in 90 seconds, and is never touched again.
